Ezekiel Elliott: Career

Collegiate year

Ezekiel Elliott attended Ohio State University and played football for the Ohio State Buckeyes Football team.

Further, he was a backup player during his rookie year and played as a gunner for the team.

However, Elliott managed to rush for 262 yards on 30 carries with two touchdowns.

The following season he became the starter for the Buckeyes and was named in the Academic All-Big Ten Conference team at the end of the season.

Meanwhile, his 220 rush yards on 20 carried for two touchdowns bagged win against Wisconsin Badgers and led Buckeyes to the first-ever College Football Playoffs.

Unfortunately, Buckeyes were thrashed by the Alabama Crimson Tides in the final.

In addition, Elliott ended his collegiate career on a high note with numerous records to his name.

NFL career

In the 2016 NFL draft, the Dallas Cowboys picked Elliott in the first round with the fourth overall draft choice.

Further, Elliott was declared the Cowboys’ leading running back before the start of the NFL 2016 season.

Subsequently, He ran for 51 yards on 20 tries in his NFL debut and scored his first NFL touchdown.

Not to mention, Elliott ended his first season as the NFL’s leading rush yards with 1,631 yards, the 47th highest season in NFL history.

Dallas Cowboys running back Elliott was named First-Team All-Pro and played in his first Pro Bowl as a consequence of his great season.

On the other hand, Elliott concluded the 2017 season with 983 yards running, seven rushing touchdowns, 269 yards receiving, and two receiving touchdowns.

In fact, his peers placed him 54th on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2018.

Moreover, Elliott led the Cowboys to the top of the NFC East and into NFC Playoffs in 2018.

In fact, Cowboys running back Ezekiel Elliott earned the best rushing award for the second time in three seasons.

Furthermore, Elliott was given a $90 million six-year contract extension on September 4, 2019, with a year remaining on his deal.

Besides, Elliott ended the 2019 season with 1,357 yards and 12 touchdowns on the ground.

In addition, he had 54 catches for 420 yards and two receiving touchdowns.

Unfortunately, Elliott was missed in his first professional NFL career game against the San Francisco 49ers in Week 15 of the 2020 season due to injury.

To sum up the 2020 season, the running back had 979 rushing yards and six rushing touchdowns, as well as 52 catches for 338 receiving yards and two receiving touchdowns.
